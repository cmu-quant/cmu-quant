Quant Talk

Speaker: Dexter Senft (Bio)
Time: Thursday Feb 17, 5:00pm & Friday Feb 18, 4:30pm
Location: Porter Hall A100 (Thursday), TCS Hall (Friday)
Description:

The Psychology of Buying

Suppose you see a house that you would love to buy. You brashly march up to the front door and ask the owner if she is willing to sell. She is! But she's so intrigued by your idea that she decides to put the house up for auction. There will be no negotiation; the best price wins. She provides basic "Zillow-type" information on the house (acreage, beds, baths, utilities, the price she paid, etc.) to all potential buyers but no more. You can't even go inside! You really want to try to buy it. What's your plan? Come hear some of the ways to approach this kind of problem.

Electronic Market Making in Foreign Exchange

The Foreign Exchange market is the largest and most liquid market in the world, with daily trading volumes in the trillions of dollars -- far larger than the global equity markets. Profit margins are razor thin, and can be as little as $10 per $million for trades between US dollars, Euros and Yen. Today, computers are used to automatically make prices and complete transactions in this market with no human involvement -- other than monitoring of the system. The speaker will describe how he and his team built such a system for a major bank.
